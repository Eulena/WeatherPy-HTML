Latitude vs. Temperature

This plot of latitude and temperature illustrates how the temperature increases as it gets closer to the equator (0 degrees latitude). Temperatures above 90 degrees farenheit (90F) are observed almost exclusively between -20 and 20 degrees latitude.

Temperatures of below 20F are extremely low and are highly concentrated in cities in the northern hemisphere between 40 and 80 degrees latitude. There were no cities in the southern hemisphere with temperatures below 40F. Cold temperatures were seemed to be influenced by date, as these weather observations were recorded in November when the northern hemisphere is closer to winter and the southern hemisphere closer to summer.
